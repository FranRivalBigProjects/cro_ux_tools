// Root causes

What Causes Cart Abandonment — And How to Fix Each

Unexpected extra costs (48% of abandonment)
Shipping costs, taxes, and fees appearing for the first time at checkout cause the largest share of abandonments. The fix is not free shipping — it's transparency. Show total estimated cost on the product page and in the cart before checkout starts. Stores that display shipping costs before checkout see 10-15% fewer abandonments than those that reveal costs at the final step.

Forced account creation (24% of abandonment)
Requiring users to create an account before purchasing adds friction and delays. The fix: offer guest checkout with an optional account creation step after order completion ("Save your details for faster checkout next time?"). Allowing guest checkout typically recovers 3-5% of abandoned sessions from this cause alone.

Complicated checkout process (17% of abandonment)
Long forms, unclear steps, and unnecessary fields increase cognitive load and drop-off. Best practice: 3 steps maximum (cart → shipping+payment → confirmation), auto-fill for returning users, address validation that reduces errors, and a visible progress indicator. Each additional checkout step adds approximately 5-8% to your abandonment rate.

Lack of trust signals near payment (18% of abandonment)
Users evaluate trustworthiness at the payment step — when they're about to share card details. Display SSL/HTTPS badge, accepted payment method logos, a brief security statement, and customer review count near the payment form. Trust signals that are visible during the payment step specifically outperform those shown only on the homepage.

Slow or expensive delivery (22% of abandonment)
Delivery speed and cost are evaluated before purchase decision. Show estimated delivery date (not just shipping time) early in the funnel. Offer multiple delivery tiers including a slower free option. Stores with 2-3 delivery options see lower abandonment than those with only one expensive option or one slow free option.
